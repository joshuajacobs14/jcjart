About JCJ

Jaspen Clay Jacobs is a queer nonbinary American artist, designer, and social media content creator who also goes by the names JC, JCJ, or Jorts and uses the pronouns they/them/their, xe/xem/xer, she/her/hers, or he/him/his.

Personal Life

Jaspen Clay Jacobs was born Joshua Carl Jacobs, in Utah. They were raised in the LDS church along with their ten siblings.

JCJ has pursued art since childhood and has taken art classes from elementary to high school. They studied Art & Design (A.A.) at Utah Valley University.

At age 19 they went on a 2-year LDS mission in the Philippines. At age 23 they came out of the closet as gay and left the LDS church. At age 27 they came out as nonbinary.

In 2015 they started selling artwork as "Art Joshua Studio", later renamed "JCJ Art & Design" in 2022. They have created and sold LGBTQ+ themed artwork and merchandise at pride festivals, markets, events, conventions, and online using Etsy and Redbubble.

Being queer and ex-mormon has been an important part of their identity as an artist. They have made artworks that reflect the hardships of growing up queer in a conservative cult. They also strive to make works that uplift the LGBT community, and advocate for marginalized groups.
