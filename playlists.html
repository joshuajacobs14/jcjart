Playlist Covers



I love music. I’ve always been fascinated by album artwork and I’ve wanted to design covers myself. I also like to make playlists on spotify so I started making covers for my playlists. Every few months I make a new playlist of my current favorite songs. For years I would struggle to come up with unique names for each playlist. For a time I would just use the year and month or season but I didn’t always feel like that was an accurate representation of the songs.

In 2021 I just started lettering my playlists starting with A at the beginning of each year (“2021 A”, “2021 B”, “2021 C”, etc). This way the playlist isn’t technically tethered to a specific month or season, but I still have a vague idea of when I made the playlist. I would design the playlist cover artwork inspired by the songs in the playlist, my emotions, personal events, current events, and my favorite design trends. This led me to think differently about my playlists and music in general. I would naturally start thinking of words that start with the playlist letter and fit the vibe of the playlist. I started to notice patterns, like C often stands for “crying”, because I’m frequently sad that time of year. Meanwhile, E stood for “energy” and “electricity” one year, but another year it stood for “empty” and “existential dread”. I never get to Z. Each year I might make more or fewer playlists.
